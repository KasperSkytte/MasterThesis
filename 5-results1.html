Enterotyping Danish Wastewater Treatment Plants

5 Results Part 1: Exploring

Filtering the samples from the Wastewater Treatment Plants (WWTPs) resulted in a remaining 622 samples from 32 WWTPs containing a total of 21728 different OTU’s. Because this is still a very large amount of data to visualise (even when using ordination), the following plots may be better viewed in the interactive online bookdown version of this report, where it is possible to zoom in the plots and hover the points for additional information. It is available at https://github.com/KasperSkytte/MasterThesis. In the following the WWTPs will be described using exploratory ordination methods (as described in Chapter 2). The differences between the WWTPs are represented by the distance between sample points if not noted otherwise. The points are colored by a unique color for each WWTP and the corresponding name of the WWTP is written at the approximate center of all the sample points from the particular WWTP.

The overall differences between the WWTP’s are illustrated in Figure 5.1 by Principal Components Analysis, where the hellinger transformation has been used to give low abundant OTUs less weight. Clearly, there seem to be many similarities between the WWTPs as the groups of samples tend to overlap. It is also possible to identify global patterns by groups of WWTPs (groups of groups), however, which seem to be related at least partly. The largest dissimilarities observed in an ordination plot are points positioned diagonally of one another, which is the case with for example the Ribe and Bjergmarken WWTPs. Imagine a line from Ribe towards Bjergmarken. Two groups of WWTPs can then be identified by separating the line with a perpendicular line in the middle in the direction of Fornaes-Haderslev, with one group on each side of it. Of course, this is a rough way of clustering the WWTPs, but along this diagonal seems to be the greatest variation in species composition and/or abundances between the WWTPs. The following two PCA and NMDS plots show very similar groupings of the WWTPs. With both ordination methods the WWTP’s Ribe, Hjoerring, Esbjerg W+E, Viborg, Randers, Aars, Horsens, Hirtshals, Ringkoebing and Moerke seem to be clustered together positioned opposite of Odense NE+NW, Bjergmarken, Egaa, Middelfart, Aalborg E+W, Soenderborg, Avedoere, Aabenraa, Viby, Ejby Moelle, Aaby, Marselisborg and Kolding. However, they seem to disagree on Skive and Soeholt, and Fornaes, Boeslum, Haderslev, Soeholt, Kolding and Lundtofte seem to be in between on PCA+RDA, but with NMDS Kolding, Lundtofte and Skive.
